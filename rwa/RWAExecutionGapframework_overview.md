## What the Execution Gap Looks Like in RWA Programs

Most regulated RWA programs do not fail at the technology layer.

They fail because governance is defined too late. Banking access is started too late. The exit strategy is written after the first investor has already subscribed. The MLRO succession plan does not exist until the regulator asks for it.

The Execution Gap opens quietly — often before anyone names it as a problem.

---

## Framework Scope — 7 Operational Layers

Every RWA program requires structured delivery across seven layers, in sequence. The sequence matters. A layer skipped does not disappear — it relocates the problem to a later, more expensive stage.

| Layer | Focus |
|---|---|
| L0 — Foundation & Operating Model | Governance, RACI, OKR framework, budget management, decision rights |
| L1 — Regulatory & Legal | MiCA/CASP, DORA, eWpG, MLRO, AML program, multi-jurisdiction |
| L2 — Structure & Contracts | SPV, token classification, vendor MSA, SLA framework, decision tracking |
| L3 — Technical Architecture | SC design, custody architecture, oracle selection, vendor governance |
| L4 — Build & Audit | SC development, SC audit, pen test, DORA ICT, SAST/DAST |
| L5 — Integration & Testing | End-to-end, settlement, PoR baseline, UAT, rollback plan, gate sign-off |
| L6 — Go-Live & Operations | G-OPS gate, hypercare, KPI/KRI monitoring, change & incident management |

## What a Complete RWA Program Requires

**25 delivery plans** — each with a defined initiation layer, named owner, and timing. Three plans are consistently started too late:

- **Banking Strategy** — banking screening takes 8–20 weeks. The clock starts from Week 1, not after the platform is built.
- **Exit & Wind-Down Strategy** — must be disclosed in the Offering Memorandum before any investor subscribes.
- **Key Person Succession Plan** — NCA approval for MLRO replacement takes 4–8 weeks. It must exist before departure.

**11 risk categories** — standard risk registers miss the ones that actually cause delays. The category most consistently absent: soft risks.

Soft risks that cause delays without appearing in RAG reports: stakeholder pseudo-consensus · unclear ownership at function boundaries · slow cross-functional decisions · regulatory fear culture · blockchain/banking knowledge gap between teams.

**20 vendor categories** — from oracle and SC audit through custody, banking, KYC/AML, and market maker. Each with lead times that affect the critical path if not sequenced correctly.

**12 reporting streams** — running in parallel from go-live. Each with a named owner and a hard regulatory deadline.
