## Who Needs a CASP Licence

Under MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation), any entity providing crypto-asset services in the EU on a professional basis requires authorisation as a CASP. For a tokenisation infrastructure provider, this typically covers:

- Custody and administration of crypto-assets on behalf of clients
- Operation of a trading platform for crypto-assets
- Exchange of crypto-assets for funds or other crypto-assets
- Transfer services for crypto-assets
- Portfolio management and advice

The licence is held by the **legal entity** providing the service — not the technology platform. Where an infrastructure provider enables multiple issuers, the provider holds the CASP licence; issuers operate under it.

## Competent Authority (NCA) — Jurisdiction Selection

| Jurisdiction | NCA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Germany | BaFin | eWpG-regulated electronic securities; strong for bond tokenisation |
| Luxembourg | CSSF | Preferred for fund tokenisation; EU passporting friendly |
| Ireland | CBI | Common for PSP and payments infrastructure |
| France | AMF | Active MiCA early adopter |
| Netherlands | AFM + DNB | Dual-authority structure |
| UAE (VARA) | VARA | Non-EU; separate regime — ARVA licence, not MiCA CASP |

**Jurisdiction selection is a Layer 1 gate decision.** It determines regulatory timeline, capital requirements, and issuer onboarding scope. It cannot be changed without a material programme change.

## What Infrastructure Providers Get Wrong

**Starting too late.** Licensing preparation requires IT documentation, compliance infrastructure, and governance structures that take months to build. Starting at L3 means arriving at G-OPS without a licence.

**Treating it as a legal workstream only.** CASP licensing requires IT evidence (DORA-aligned ICT framework, audit logs, BCP/DRP), operational evidence (custody model, onboarding procedures), and financial evidence (capital, projections). It is a cross-functional delivery workstream.

**Underestimating NCA questions.** Every NCA question pauses the assessment clock. Incomplete or delayed responses extend the timeline by weeks. Preparation quality determines timeline, not NCA speed.

**One jurisdiction only.** If the primary NCA application is rejected or materially delayed, there is no fallback. Parallel assessment of a secondary jurisdiction is a programme risk mitigation, not a cost.
